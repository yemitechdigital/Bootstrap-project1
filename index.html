Frequently Asked Questions

The course is designed to be completed over a span of 7 months, with a commitment of 5-7 hour per week. Each weeks includes video lectures, reading material, and assignments that help reinforce the concepts learned. While the course is self-paced, we encourage participants to stay on track with the weekly schedule to maximize their larning experience.

By the end of the course, you will have the skills to build several web applications. Examples include a **personal portfolio website** that showcase your Projects and skills, a **dynamic e-commerce platform** with user authentication and payment integration, and a **real time chat application** using Websockets. Additionally, you will learn to create a **task management tool** similar to Trello uing ReactJS for the front end, NodeJS and ExpreeJS for the backend, and MongoDB as the database. You may also build a small applications that integrate with third-party APIs, such as a **wheather dashboard** that fetches real-time data from a whether API. The final project will be a comprehensive application that incorporates all these skills, serving a capstone to demonstrate your proficiency and creativity, providing you with a portfolio piece to show potential employers or clients.

This course is designed for beginners, so no prior programming experience is required. However, a basic understanding of how to use a computer and navigate the internet is helpful. We also recommend having a good grasp of problem-solving skills and logical thinking. All necessary software tools and resources will be provided, and you will be guided step-by-step through the setup and use of each one.

Throughout the course, you will have access to a range of support options. These include a dedicated course forum where you can ask questions and get help from instructors and fellow students, weekly live Q&A sessions, and 24/7 access to all course materials. If you encounter any issues or have questions, our support team is available via email or chat.
